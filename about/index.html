Our History

The Birth of DNC (1971)

In 1971, a group of concerned citizens came together with a vision to preserve the natural open spaces of Demarest. They identified a beautiful 55-acre parcel of land bordered by Columbus Road to the west and County Road to the east. This marked the beginning of what would become the Demarest Nature Center.

Incorporation (December 4, 1972)

On December 4, 1972, fifteen dedicated individuals officially signed the incorporation papers, formally establishing the Demarest Nature Center as a non-profit organization. Luther Lee Emerson served as our first president, leading the organization with passion and dedication.

The Lease Agreement (1973)

In 1973, the newly formed organization secured a 41-year lease for the land at the symbolic rate of $1.00 per year. While the group originally requested 77 acres, the final agreement granted 55 acres - a substantial preserve that would serve the community for generations to come.

First Cleanup Day (April 8, 1972)

Even before official incorporation, our founding members rolled up their sleeves on April 8, 1972, for the first major cleanup of the property. Working together, they filled a 40-foot dumpster with refuse, including tires, paint buckets, and even engine blocks. This effort transformed the neglected land into the natural sanctuary we cherish today.

Our Logo

The iconic Great Blue Heron that serves as our logo was originally sketched by Henry Duffy, one of our founding members. This majestic bird represents the wildlife we work to protect and the natural beauty of our preserve.

Our Mission

From our 1972 Articles of Incorporation, our purposes are:

1

To acquire or lease undeveloped lands and establish thereon educational building(s) for the purpose of enhancing the understanding and appreciation of our natural environment.

2

To develop natural history and conservation education programs in cooperation with schools, colleges, hospitals, and youth groups, fostering environmental awareness across all ages.

3

To cooperate with national, state, county, municipal, and private natural resource agencies in the conservation and preservation of our shared natural heritage.

A Volunteer-Run Organization

Demarest Nature Center is a 501(c)(3) non-profit organization run entirely by dedicated volunteers. We receive no funding from the Borough of Demarest, relying instead on the generosity of our members, donors, and the countless hours contributed by our volunteers.

What We Sponsor

Throughout the year, we offer a variety of programs and events for the community:

  • Lectures on native plants and ecology
  • Family-friendly guided hikes
  • Maple syrup making demonstrations
  • Bird watching sessions and bird counts
  • Birdhouse and bird feeder building workshops
  • Mushroom foraging walks with experts
  • Community trail walks
  • Children's scavenger hunts
  • Annual Oktoberfest Craft Show
  • Nature photography workshops
